We make open source projects sustainable

Apply the Open Compensation Token license (OCTL) to your opensource project and start getting paid for your work.

What does it?

The Open Compensation Token License (OCTL) promotes sustainability in open-source projects by allowing creators to monetize their work. It provides a unique licensing mechanism where users of the project must acquire compensation tokens to comply with the license terms. The OCTL is designed to ensure contributors get fairly paid while still supporting non-commercial and community-based usage for free. It bridges the gap between corporate profit-making and open-source contribution, guaranteeing fair compensation for developers if corporations use or resell their work commercially. The license offers transparency, ownership proof, and an opportunity for developers to thrive financially while improving the open-source ecosystem. OCTL also encourages non-profits and growing the OS community without barriers.

Whats in it for you?

The **Open Compensation Token License (OCTL)** offers significant benefits to maintainers, developers, and project owners, addressing key challenges in open-source development: 1. **Fair Compensation**: Maintainers get paid when their work is commercially used, ensuring they can financially sustain themselves while focusing on developing their projects. 2. **Control Over Licensing**: The OCTL empowers project owners to set transparent terms for commercial use while still allowing free use for non-commercial contributions. 3. **Encourages Contribution**: Developers no longer need to sacrifice financial stability to maintain an open-source project, motivating continuous updates, improvements, and innovation. 4. **Increased Recognition**: The token model provides clear attribution and proof of ownership, helping maintainers gain visibility and respect in the community. 5. **Sustainable Future**: By monetizing corporate use, maintainers avoid burnout and can thrive while contributing to technology and society. The license ensures maintainers are rewarded fairly without compromising their open-source principles
